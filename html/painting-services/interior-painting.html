Common Wall Painting Mistakes

Here are a few common mistakes that can occur when you're painting the interior walls of your home:

  • Not mixing the paint thoroughly causing variations in tone. If you do not mix all of the paint into one batch you will notice slight color and sheen differences on the walls that will be very noticeable.

  • Not priming any stains which will affect the appearance of the finished wall. This is basically mandatory if you are painting a light color over a dark color. Always prime the walls with white primer before painting the interior of your home.

  • Not pressing in the edges of the tape allowing paint to bleed behind it. You have to press the tape down firmly on the edges where it meets the wall. You need you create a strong seal between the tape and the wall to ensure to paint can seep and leak underneath it.

  • Stopping work in the middle of a wall causing an overlap mark which will be difficult to hide later. Don't let the paint dry before you continue painting over it. The dried paint will leave it's indent under the new paint because that area was not able to mix with the new layer, which causes it to look overlapped.

  • Allowing the paint to get too thick throughout the day which can cause brush marks on the finished surface. You don't want to see brush marks on your nice wall!

Tips On How To Paint Interior Walls In Your Home

  • Using High-Quality Paints: When painting the interior walls of your home, you really do get what you pay for. Low quality paints don't hold up very well and over time may chip or peel. They often need multiple coats just to get an even coverage. You will be staring at these walls for years so it's important to use the best quality paints from the start. Here at Henry's Painting and Drywall, we use only the best products to use on your interior walls.

  • Use Canvas Drop Cloths Instead of Plastic: Many professionals always choose canvas drop cloths for painting inside of homes. They are durable, thick, and absorbs the paint. Some of these cloths have a rubberized layer on the backside so it grips the floor better and keeps any spilled paint from seeping through. The heavier the canvas, the better it absorbs without the paint seeping through.

  • Use 18in Rollers Instead of 12in: If you are painting one or two interior walls in your home, a 12 inch roller will work just fine. But if you have a much bigger job on your hands, you might want to look into an 18 inch roller to cover more square feet faster.
